Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 20, 2014

1. Introduction

Courtroom Assistance Program, a program provided by the America Family Law Center ("we," "us," or "our"), is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website, affiliated websites, or use our services.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information

We may collect personal information that is publicly available or that you voluntarily provide to us when you:

  • Fill out forms on our website or elsewhere
  • Request information about our services
  • Subscribe to our newsletter or communications
  • Interact with our website or services
  • Interact with any of our AI Agents
  • Interact with any of our staff or personnel

This information may include your name, email address, phone number, mailing address, and any other information.

2.2 Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our website or any of our affiliated websites, we may automatically collect certain information about you, your device and browsing behavior, including:

  • IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent on pages
  • Referring website addresses
  • Device information
  • Files opened or viewed

2.3 Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies to collect information about your browsing activities. Cookies are small data files stored on your device that help us improve our website, our services, and your experience. You can control cookie settings through your browser, but disabling cookies may limit your ability to use certain features of our website.

3. How We Use Your Information

We may use the information we collect for any lawful purpose, including but not limited to:

  • Providing, operating, and maintaining our services
  • Responding to your inquiries and requests
  • Sending you information about our programs and services
  • Improving our website and services
  • Analyzing usage patterns and trends
  • Marketing and promotional purposes
  • Communicating with you about updates, news, and other information
  • Preventing fraud and enhancing security
  • Complying with legal obligations
  • Any other purpose for which you provide consent
  • Any other purpose we deem necessary or beneficial to our organization or our affiliates

4. Sharing Your Information

We reserve the right to share your information with:

  • America Family Law Center and its affiliated organizations
  • Service providers who assist us in operating our website and services
  • Legal and regulatory authorities when required by law
  • Third parties in connection with a merger, sale, or transfer of assets
  • Any other third party with your consent or as permitted by law
  • Marketing partners and other organizations for promotional purposes

5. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. We may retain your information indefinitely for any lawful purpose.

6. Data Security

While we implement reasonable security measures to protect your information, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your information.

7. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of these external sites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit.

8. Children's Privacy

Our services are not intended for individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have collected information from a child, we will take steps to delete such information.

9. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information, including the right to access, correct, or delete your information. To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information provided below. Please note that we reserve the right to deny such requests if permitted or required by law.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We reserve the right to modify this Privacy Policy at any time without prior notice. All changes will be effective immediately upon posting to our website. Your continued use of our website and services following any changes constitutes your acceptance of the revised Privacy Policy.

11. Consent

By using our website and services, you consent to this Privacy Policy and agree that we may collect, use, and share your information as described herein for any lawful purpose we deem appropriate.

12. Contact Us

If you have questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy, please contact us through our Contact Us page.

Disclaimer: This Privacy Policy is provided for informational purposes. Nothing in this policy creates a contractual relationship or limits our ability to use your information as permitted by applicable law.
